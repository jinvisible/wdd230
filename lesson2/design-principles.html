Visual Hierarchy

Apple

apple.com

Webpage of Apple is a great example of showing the 'Visual Hierarchy'. It considers reading patterns, uses bigger elements so users notice them more easily, uses colors at right places to draw the eye, and uses white space to point the important elements.

Hick's Law

Quora

quora.com

Quora's webpage used perfect elements of 'Hick's Law'. It has two big visible buttons which help users simplify their decision-making process.

Fitt's Law

Google

google.com

'Fitt's Law' has a very simple rule of design and Google is one of the best example to show it. Every important buttons on google are so close to each other so the users don't have to move that much to click any of those buttons. Also, the main search bar is in good spot so users can easily access to the search bar.
